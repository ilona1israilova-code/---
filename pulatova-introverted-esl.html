Brief description

The article provides a theoretical and analytical overview of international approaches to developing ESL speaking skills, with a particular focus on introverted learners in secondary schools. It draws on the works of Nunan, Larsen-Freeman, Skehan, Littlewood, Dörnyei, Bax, and Scrivener to examine task-based learning, learner-centred curricula, motivational frameworks and technology integration, and proposes a multi-stage model that reduces anxiety and supports gradual participation in oral interaction.
